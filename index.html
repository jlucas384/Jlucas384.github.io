Lacrosse Website

Sport

Lacrosse was a sport created by the Indigenous people. It originates from the northeastern side of North America. There were many tribes that helped popularize the sports, but the Iroquois have been the most successful with it. During the beggining of lacrosse it was more than just a game to the native americans. It had a cultural and spiritual meaning.

Lacrosse Roots

Lacrosse started off as a simple game. In the early days it was played with a wooden stick and a small rubber ball. The fields used to be any size ranging from a hundred yards to a mile long. The size of the field and rules of the game varied between tribes but it normally stayed similar. The golden rule was that there were two teams and the object of the game was to score more goals than the other team.

Iriquois Roots

The iroquois tribe which is also known as the Haudenosaunee people fell in love with the game the quickest. Lacrosse held a deep spiritual and cultural meaning towards them. The game was viewed as a gift from the creator to them. They all enjoyed playing the game and it was a way to get away from everyday life. They held ceremonies, rituals, and it brought a social aspect between them, it was seen as a way to strengthen the community.
